# SteadyMind Pilot Shortlist

Use this sheet to compare possible pilot users before inviting the first 2-3 people.

The goal is not to find “perfect” users.
The goal is to choose people who are likely to use the product honestly, on phone, and give specific feedback.

## How To Use This Sheet

1. Write down 3-5 real candidate names.
2. Score each person from 1-5 on the criteria below.
3. Add a short note about any risk or concern.
4. Choose the best 2-3 overall, not just the most enthusiastic people.

## Scoring Criteria

Score each candidate from 1-5 on:

- Reflection fit
  Are they naturally open to reflection and self-development?

- Phone-first fit
  Are they likely to actually use this on phone in real life?

- Feedback quality
  Are they likely to give specific, useful feedback rather than only broad praise?

- Follow-through
  Are they likely to use it for 3-5 days instead of trying it once and disappearing?

- Emotional fit
  Are they a good fit for a calm reflection product, without expecting therapy or a polished mass-market app?

## Selection Rule

The first 2-3 pilot users should ideally:

- score well across most categories, not just one
- include at least one person who is likely to give very concrete feedback
- include at least one person who is likely to use the app quietly and naturally, not only “test” it

Avoid choosing only:

- very enthusiastic people who may overpraise it
- people who are unlikely to return after day one
- people who are a poor fit for reflection products in general

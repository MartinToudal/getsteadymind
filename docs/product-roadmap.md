# SteadyMind Product Roadmap

This roadmap is intentionally outcome-based rather than feature-heavy.
It is designed to support a product that is already live, used regularly, and still learning from real usage.

## Roadmap Logic

The goal is not to ship the most features.
The goal is to strengthen SteadyMind as a daily mental fitness practice that people can realistically keep.

The roadmap therefore follows four product phases:

1. Make daily use frictionless
2. Make the value feel clearer
3. Make the method stronger
4. Make the product ready for pilots and scale

## Phase 1: Daily Use

### Outcome

SteadyMind becomes easy to return to every day, especially on mobile and especially on ordinary, imperfect days.

### Why this phase matters

If the product does not fit naturally into a daily routine, nothing else matters.
At this stage, habit and usability are more important than adding more scope.

### Focus areas

- make Home feel like a calm daily start point
- reduce friction in mobile navigation and reading flow
- make Daily Pulse feel easy and coherent
- make guided sessions feel approachable, even when the user is mentally tired
- support consistency without adding pressure

### Example initiatives

- improve mobile spacing and hierarchy
- sharpen the day-start flow on Home
- continue improving prompt logic in Daily Pulse
- tune copy so the experience feels lighter without becoming vague
- refine re-entry behavior for users who fall out of rhythm

### Success signals

- the founder uses the product consistently in real life
- mobile use feels natural enough for morning or evening routine use
- fewer moments of confusion or hesitation in the daily flow

### Done For Now

Phase 1 is done for now when:

- daily use feels reliable and low-friction on phone
- Home, Daily Pulse, and the next session are easy to find and start
- the founder can use the product for multiple weeks without major usability friction
- new polish requests are mostly refinements, not blockers to the daily habit

## Phase 2: Felt Value

### Outcome

Users can more clearly feel what the product is doing for them.

### Why this phase matters

Mental fitness only becomes compelling if people can notice change over time.
Without that, the app risks feeling like structured journaling but not a meaningful practice.

### Focus areas

- make progress feel useful, not decorative
- help users understand what is changing over time
- connect reflection to real-life clarity and steadiness
- surface gentle insight without becoming noisy or “AI coach” flavored

### Example initiatives

- improve how trends are explained
- add simple summaries of recent development
- make Progress support interpretation, not just display
- strengthen the relationship between check-ins, sessions, and what the user learns

### Success signals

- users can describe what SteadyMind is helping them improve
- progress views feel meaningful and not just nice-looking
- the user feels more clear, less scattered, and more grounded after a few weeks

### Done For Now

Phase 2 is done for now when:

- the product makes progress feel interpretable, not decorative
- users can describe the value in their own words
- Progress supports reflection instead of creating confusion
- the product feels like it creates real benefit over time, not just daily activity

## Phase 3: Stronger Method

### Outcome

SteadyMind feels more clearly like a method for training the inner self, not just a set of prompts.

### Why this phase matters

This is where the product becomes more distinctive.
The user should feel they are practicing specific inner capabilities over time.

### Focus areas

- make the SteadyMind Method more explicit
- clarify what each phase trains
- improve pacing across Foundation 30
- help sessions feel cumulative and intentional

### Example initiatives

- introduce clearer method language in sessions
- strengthen the link between Awareness, Clarity, Direction, Momentum, and Identity
- introduce soft pacing for Foundation 30
- revisit prompts that feel too abstract too early
- make session completion feel more connected to action and integration

### Success signals

- users describe the product as training, not just reflection
- the method feels coherent from one session to the next
- early sessions feel accessible while later sessions still carry depth

### Done For Now

Phase 3 is done for now when:

- the SteadyMind Method is visible in the product, not only in internal thinking
- Foundation 30 feels cumulative and intentionally paced
- the five mental fitness capabilities are reflected clearly enough in the experience
- users can sense that they are training something, not just answering prompts

## Phase 4: Pilot Readiness

### Outcome

SteadyMind is stable and clear enough to be used by a small group of external pilot users.

### Why this phase matters

Pilot usage is where product learning becomes more trustworthy.
But it only makes sense after daily usage, felt value, and method clarity are reasonably strong.

### Focus areas

- identify the minimum level of product robustness required
- reduce avoidable confusion in onboarding and daily use
- prepare the product for a small but real audience
- tighten setup, operations, and trust signals

### Example initiatives

- define the pilot standard for product quality
- improve onboarding clarity
- connect custom domains when ready
- introduce a proper migration workflow
- extend test coverage beyond current E2E flows

### Success signals

- 2-3 pilot users can onboard and use the product without hand-holding
- core flows work reliably on mobile
- the product communicates enough value to support continued usage

### Done For Now

Phase 4 is done for now when:

- a small set of pilot users can use the product with minimal support
- mobile, login, check-in, session, and progress flows are stable in real usage
- the team is comfortable sharing the product without needing to apologize for core roughness
- product learning starts to come from external users, not only the founder

## Native App Timing

The native app should be treated as an expansion step, not as the way to discover basic product truth.

### Why not now

Right now, the biggest questions are still about:

- habit strength
- felt value
- method clarity
- what actually makes the product worth returning to

Those questions can be answered in the current web product.
Building native too early would likely increase scope faster than it increases learning.

### When a native app starts to make sense

A native app becomes a good next move when these things are true:

- daily mobile web usage already feels meaningfully useful
- the core loop is stable and worth preserving
- the team knows which moments matter most on mobile
- there is a clear reason native solves a real problem better than web

### Likely roadmap placement

The native app most likely belongs after Phase 2, or during late Phase 3 if:

- the product value is proven
- the method is strong enough to preserve
- mobile usage is central enough to justify a dedicated app experience

### What a native app should improve

If SteadyMind gets a native app later, it should improve things like:

- a more natural daily return experience
- faster mobile entry into check-in and session flow
- stronger feeling of a dedicated personal practice
- better long-term retention on phone

It should not be built just because apps feel more “real.”
It should be built because native helps the daily mental fitness habit work better.

## Current Recommendation

SteadyMind is currently between Phase 1 and early Phase 2.

That means the right near-term priorities are:

- frictionless daily mobile use
- stronger day-start experience
- clearer progress meaning
- continued learning from real use

Not the right near-term priorities:

- more programs
- more features for breadth
- heavy analytics or gamification
- broad pilot rollout before the core habit feels solid

## Near-Term Product Bets

These are the most important product bets right now:

1. If Home becomes a better daily starting point, consistency will improve.
2. If Progress becomes easier to understand, the product will feel more valuable.
3. If Daily Pulse stays lightweight but coherent, users will keep returning.
4. If Foundation 30 feels paced and methodical, SteadyMind will feel like real mental training.

## Roadmap Principle

Every next step should support one of these:

- make the daily habit easier to keep
- make the value easier to feel
- make the method easier to understand
- make the product safer to share with real users
